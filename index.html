What it solves

A high-touch extension of care—without adding administrative weight.

The application replaces scattered handouts, follow-up texts, exercise links, marketing tools, and progress spreadsheets with one simple patient and practitioner experience.

01

Patients know what to do

Each patient receives a clear recovery plan, exercise visuals, goals, reminders, and a fast daily check-in.

02

Practitioners see the trend

Completion, comfort, goals, barriers, and structured flags are organized before the next appointment.

03

The clinic stays connected

Clinic Connect creates educational newsletters, clinic articles, service updates, and reusable social media content.

How a real login would be added

A
Firebase Authentication

Google sign-in, email and password, or secure email links with verification and session protection.

B
Role and data binding

Each account is mapped to a practitioner or patient profile and limited to the correct records.

C
Automatic redirect

The application reads the role and loads either the clinical dashboard or personal recovery timeline.

The sole proprietor pitch

“Increase patient retention by eliminating administrative friction.”

Recovery Companion is not a compliance checklist. It is a high-touch extension of hands-on care. It replaces physical handouts, scattered texts, exercise emails, and separate marketing software with a single portal that supports follow-through in under a minute a day.

⏱

Protect practitioner time

Structured questions and automatic flags prepare the practitioner before the next appointment.

↗

Improve continuity

Patients can always return to the correct exercise, goal, message, or educational article.

◎

Reduce subscriptions

Recovery plans, newsletters, clinic announcements, and analytics live in one system.

Production roadmap

What changes before real patients use it.

1. Secure identity

Replace demo switching with verified accounts, protected routes, and role-based permissions.

2. Protected data

Add Firestore security rules, audit history, consent controls, and clinic-specific boundaries.

3. Clinical and legal review

Review privacy, scope, emergency messaging, accessibility, email consent, and hosting before launch.
